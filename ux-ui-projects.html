About My Work

In my web development works, I place a heavy focus on curating beautiful applications and websites that adhere to UX/UI Design principles. This ensures that users have a seamless and enjoyable experience on every website I develope. When I address free lance web development, I place the company first and turn the company's dreams into a reality. All of my web development projects are built from raw code with the implementation of Bootstrap elements.

In order to ensure I create seamless applications and websites, I place a heavy emphasis on User Experience Reasearch. In my research projects, I have conducted user interviews, developed user stories, addressed universal and local issues, and more.

Bubblegum Babe Swim

This project involved creating a polished user interface mock-up for Bubblegum Babe Swim, a small online swimwear shop. The site includes a range of functional pages, such as New Arrivals, Shop, User Login, and Contact. Each page is designed with interactivity in mind, featuring hover effects on links and images to enhance user engagement.

The design emphasizes both usability and aesthetics, ensuring a seamless shopping experience for users. The layout is carefully curated to maintain a clean, appealing visual style while prioritizing user-centered design principles, making it both functional and visually captivating.

Bytes News

Designed in Figma, this is the prototype for a news application I begin development with a team. We used considerate research to curate a news app that appeals to younger audiences such as Generation Z. We included accessibility features such as light mode and dark mode, text size adjustment, and screen readers. We also included an article verification feature that uses AI to find the similarity of the article to other articles and to search for written biases.

This application is created to be an accessbile and trustworthy news source that is enjoyable for users to use. Without the cluttering of advertisments and the fast-scrolling technology, users can consume news at a rater faster than ever before

Boarderline Ski and Snowboard Club

This website was developed for Boarderline, the official ski and snowboard club at James Madison University. Its primary purpose is to serve the current members while also reaching out to potential new members interested in joining the club. The site features engaging content that highlights club activities, events, and membership benefits.

In developing the site, special attention was given to the target audience, ensuring it appealed to both experienced club members and newcomers looking to explore ski and snowboard opportunities. The design prioritizes clear navigation, event updates, and an inviting tone, making it easy for prospective members to learn about the club and get involved. The website also supports the club’s mission to foster community and excitement among outdoor enthusiasts.

Bytes: Providing Quick News for Younger Generations Application Research

This project is in progress
Scheduled Completion: December 2024

Bytes is a research and development project focused on creating a news application designed for younger audiences. By incorporating fast-scrolling technology similar to what users experience on popular social media platforms like TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and X (formerly Twitter), Bytes delivers daily news in a format that resonates with the habits and preferences of its target audience.

Our approach involved conducting comprehensive interviews and surveys with a diverse range of participants. Through careful analysis of the data collected, we gained insights into what users want, shaping the app's design and functionality to meet those needs.

Safe Sobers Application Design Requirements Research

This project focused on researching and designing a sober ride application specifically for college students at James Madison University. The idea emerged after observing unsafe sober rides being coordinated through a public GroupMe chat and noticing rising costs of ride-share services like Lyft and Uber. I identified a need for an alternative solution that prioritizes both student safety and affordability.

The design was shaped by research conducted on college students who frequently use ride-share services and the public sober rides GroupMe. Based on their feedback, the design requirements were established. The hypothetical application, Safe Sobers, would connect students with verified, background-checked student drivers. One key feature is that the ride costs would be set by the drivers, not the app, ensuring affordability. Safe Sobers would not only provide a safer ride option but also offer students a flexible job opportunity with control over their working hours.
